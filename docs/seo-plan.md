## 2. How Mexicans Actually Find Services Online

This is the most important section. The US model of "Google → website → contact form" does **not** fully apply in Mexico. Here's the actual discovery funnel:

### Primary Discovery Paths (in order of importance)

1. **Google Search** (still #1 for intentional searches)
   - Most Mexicans use Google by default on Android phones
   - Searches are often in Spanish, even for English-language services
   - Mobile-optimized pages are non-negotiable

2. **WhatsApp** (the real "default phone search")
   - 92-93% of Mexican internet users are on WhatsApp
   - Mexicans use WhatsApp to **find, evaluate, and hire** service providers
   - Word-of-mouth referrals happen via WhatsApp groups and forwarded messages
   - WhatsApp Business is used by 75%+ of Mexican businesses
   - 84% of Mexicans prefer WhatsApp for business inquiries
   - **This is the biggest difference from the US market**

3. **Facebook** (the "yellow pages" of Mexico)
   - 93 million users in Mexico — the most-used platform
   - Facebook Groups are heavily used for recommendations ("¿alguien conoce un profesor de inglés?")
   - Facebook Pages serve as the primary "website" for many small businesses
   - Facebook Marketplace and local groups are discovery channels
   - 28% of all social media interactions in Mexico happen on Facebook

4. **TikTok** (the new search engine for under-35s)
   - 58 million users, 2nd most popular platform
   - Younger Mexicans use TikTok as a search engine for recommendations
   - Short-form video content drives discovery
   - TikTok is expanding e-commerce and service discovery in Mexico

5. **Instagram** (visual discovery and credibility)
   - Used to validate that a service/provider is legitimate
   - Stories and Reels drive engagement
   - DMs are used for initial contact (similar to WhatsApp)

6. **Google Maps / Google Business Profile**
   - Important for local credibility even for online services
   - Reviews on Google Maps carry significant weight

7. **YouTube**
   - 74.1% reach; Mexicans consume heavy video content
   - Used for learning and research
   - Tutorial content drives trust

### The Mexican Discovery Funnel (vs. US)

```
US Model:                    Mexico Model:
Google Search → Website      Google/WhatsApp/Facebook/TikTok →
→ Contact Form → Email       WhatsApp Message → Quick Response →
→ Schedule Call              Book / Pay via link
```

**Critical insight:** In Mexico, the **first contact is almost always a message, not a form fill.** WhatsApp is the primary conversion channel. Your site already has a WhatsApp CTA — this is correct and should be emphasized even more.

## 7. AI Search Optimization (AEO/GEO)

This is the new frontier. AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini are changing how people find answers — including service providers.

### 7.1 What Is AEO?

**Answer Engine Optimization (AEO)** — also called GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) — is the practice of structuring content so AI systems (Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, etc.) cite and reference your content when answering user queries.

Unlike traditional SEO (ranking in blue links), AEO is about **being the source that AI quotes**.

### 7.2 Why AEO Matters for Mexico

- Google AI Overviews are rolling out globally, including LATAM
- ChatGPT usage is growing rapidly in Mexico
- Perplexity and other AI search tools are gaining adoption among tech workers (your exact target audience)
- Mexican professionals in tech are **early adopters** of AI tools
- By 2026, AI-powered search visitors are predicted to grow significantly for service-related queries

### 7.3 AEO Tactics for This Site

#### Structure Content for AI Citation

1. **Use clear Q&A format** — AI systems love direct answers
   - The FAQ section is already well-structured for this
   - Add more FAQ-style content throughout the site

2. **Write concise, direct answers** — AI prefers clear, authoritative statements
   - Example: "This program is conversation-first and confidence-first." (already on site)
   - Avoid fluffy marketing language — AI skips it

3. **Use structured data (schema markup)** — helps AI understand your content
   - FAQPage schema
   - Service schema
   - Person schema (for the tutor)
   - LocalBusiness schema

4. **Build topical authority** — AI cites sources that demonstrate expertise
   - The level pages (A0-B2) demonstrate structured expertise
   - The teacher guide demonstrates methodology
   - Add more detailed content about the teaching approach

5. **Get cited by other sources** — AI models weight content that's referenced elsewhere
   - Get listed on English tutoring directories
   - Participate in relevant forums and communities
   - Create shareable content (guides, checklists)

6. **Use natural language** — write the way people ask questions
   - "How do I stop translating English in my head?"
   - "What's the best way to practice English conversation online?"
   - "How can I improve my English for remote work?"

#### AEO-Optimized Content Examples

Create pages/content that directly answer common questions:

```
Q: ¿Cuál es la mejor forma de practicar inglés conversación en línea?
A: La mejor forma es con inmersión conversacional asistida por IA con un hablante nativo. 
   Las sesiones se enfocan en comunicación real, no en ejercicios de gramática.
```

```
Q: How do I stop translating every sentence in my head?
A: Through conversational immersion. When you're in an English-only environment with 
   AI support for when you get stuck, your brain gradually learns to think directly 
   in English instead of translating from Spanish.
```

## 8. Voice Search in Mexico

Voice search is growing in Mexico, driven by:

- **Android default:** Google Assistant is built into most Mexican phones
- **Spanish voice search:** Mexicans increasingly use voice search in Spanish
- **Hands-free convenience:** Especially while driving, cooking, or multitasking
- **Lower literacy barriers:** Voice search is easier for some demographics

### Voice Search Optimization Tips

1. **Use conversational, natural language** — voice queries are longer and more conversational
   - Written: "clases inglés en línea"
   - Voice: "¿dónde puedo tomar clases de inglés en línea con un profesor nativo?"

2. **Target question-based keywords**
   - "¿cómo puedo mejorar mi inglés?"
   - "¿cuánto cuesta un profesor de inglés en línea?"
   - "¿dónde encuentro un profesor de inglés nativo en México?"

3. **Use FAQ format** — voice assistants pull from FAQ-style content

4. **Optimize for local intent** — include "en México," "en línea," "para mexicanos"

5. **Keep answers concise** — voice assistants read short, direct answers

## 15. Schema Markup Recommendations

Add these JSON-LD schemas to relevant pages:

### Homepage
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "Service",
  "name": "Inmersión en Inglés con Mauricio Tellez",
  "description": "Inmersión conversacional en inglés asistida por IA con un hablante nativo de EE.UU. para profesionales en México.",
  "provider": { "@type": "Person", "name": "Mauricio Tellez" },
  "areaServed": { "@type": "Country", "name": "Mexico" },
  "serviceType": "English Language Tutoring",
  "availableLanguage": ["Spanish", "English"]
}
```

### FAQ Schema (Homepage)
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "¿Necesito inglés perfecto?",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "No. Las sesiones se adaptan a tu nivel. El único requisito es que quieras hablar más."
      }
    }
  ]
}
```

### Level Pages (BreadcrumbList)
```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "BreadcrumbList",
  "itemListElement": [
    { "@type": "ListItem", "position": 1, "name": "Inicio", "item": "https://english-tutor-mexico.pages.dev/" },
    { "@type": "ListItem", "position": 2, "name": "Guía del Estudiante", "item": "https://english-tutor-mexico.pages.dev/student-guide" },
    { "@type": "ListItem", "position": 3, "name": "A0 — Inglés de Supervivencia", "item": "https://english-tutor-mexico.pages.dev/levels/a0" }
```
